Annoying Reason


25/04/2022
 

You may have read previously around the site that I historically lost project files for music and generally wasn’t all that careful with keeping them and so on. Minus loosing a bunch when a hard drive died. The main reason though was laziness. You see, the first DAW I used, Reason 5, was unable to export stems.

What is a stem? A stem is one of the individual isolated tracks of a song, for example the drums. Or the vocals. Songs are made of many many layers and the stems are all of these individual layers. Reason 5 could only export the final result. In order to get the stems you would need to select one track, export it, select the next track, export it - it was tedious, essentially. Doing it one by one rather than pushing a button and pressing “go”.

However I still kept most of the Reason Project files - so if I need to get anything I could.

Fast forward to 2022 and I decide I should probably export all of the stems for the Reason tracks - just in case. If the original project file corrupts or gets lost at the very least the audio waveforms exist. It was at this point that I started to hate Reason.

Reason 12, which is what the current version is, has taken a fairly asinine turn from what it used to be - for example I used to always, by default “detach sequencer window” - the sequencer is the part that has all of the midi tracks, note lanes and stuff. Can’t do that now, for reasons I cannot work out. You can detach the “rack” which contains all of the instruments and you can detach the “mixer” which frees up the sequencer window a little bit, but now you’ve got several windows open - it’s cluttered and pointlessly convoluted.

Then there is exporting stems. Which it does! Hooray. Except if your project files are older or what it calls “legacy” files, which all of mine are. Then it won’t export stems. Which is made trickier by the fact that on the mixer window, there are no transport controls (play, pause, etc). So you have to flick backwards and forwards between windows to do that (usually space bar works as start and stop but for me, on the mixer window, did nothing). But once you’ve done all of that, then you can export all of the stems:

I’ll be honest after about doing 3 songs I gave up again. It wanted me to locate a bunch of ReFills (extra sounds and such) and I don’t know where I stored them and I lost patience.

Reason 5 had its limitations. You couldn’t record audio into it, so no importing of audio tracks, recording of vocals or instruments etc. A workaround I used was to chop audio files up and trigger them with midi on something like Kong. I believe there was some separate program that integrated with Reason and filled that gap, but I never used it. It also didn’t support VSTs until version 9 or so? Reason 5 couldn’t bounce mixer tracks.

But for all the features they’ve added and things it can do … about the best thing they’ve done is make Reason a VST. At this point I would rather use just the instruments in a different sequencer that visually makes more sense, has a better, less cluttered work flow etc. The downside is you can’t just get the VST on its own. The Rack it’s called. It’s part of the full Reason program. Which is a shame, in my opinion. I know I would almost certainly prefer a VST of Reason instruments over the full sequencer program, especially in its current state.
